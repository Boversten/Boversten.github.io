I am passionate about space exploration and my dream is to develop commercial spaceflights and enable humans traveling to deep space in the near future. My past research focused on safe resilient control for autonomous systems using safety certificates, loss of performance quantitation of a control system under loss of control authority with quantitative resilience, computationally efficient method for optimal facility placement in free-form non-convex polygons with distance constraints, and spacecraft trajectory tracking and parameter estimation around a splitting contact binary asteroid using indirect adaptive control. I received my B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 2021. In my free time, you can find me playing hard-core video games, particularly products from Hidetaka Miyazaki and Monster Hunter series, watching Italian Serie A soccer, writing chinese calligraphy, or exploring Bilibili.

Publications

Spacecraft Trajectory Tracking and Parameter Estimation Around a Splitting Contact Binary Asteroid
Acta Astronautica March 2020
Tiago Silva Jean-Baptiste Bouvier Kathleen Xu Dr. Masatoshi Hirabayashi Dr. Koki Ho

Increasing interest in asteroid mining and in-situ resource utilization will lead to an increase in asteroid surface operations. The geophysical properties of asteroids are often unknown and play a significant role in the resulting gravitational fields. Surface operations such as mining may significantly alter the asteroid’s structure or, in the case of contact binary asteroids, cause the asteroid to split depending on the rotational condition. The coupled problem of estimating unknown parameters of a splitting contact-binary system and controlling a spacecraft’s trajectory in the system’s vicinity is investigated. An indirect adaptive control scheme is utilized to simultaneously meet both objectives. The results are compared with the traditional 2-body controller and the improvement enabled by the proposed scheme is demonstrated.
